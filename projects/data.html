Background

Context: The English Wikipedia page is undoubtedly one of the richest, most active free online encyclopedias. It is also a collective construction of a virtual community where editors collaborate, argue, or gain a global reputation by their contribution to Wikipedia. When each Wikipedian edits the data, they potentially leave their own sentiments and bias in the public revision history.

Goal: With its medium as an interactive webpage, this project intends to build a narrative that starts from the macro perspective and arrives at the micro. It first maps out English Wikipedia as an international enormous project through the total number of revisions and most active editors by country, then it dives into specific content including most edited pages and what has been changed on a certain page.

Research & Design

I first researched on the goal of Wikipedia: an evolving, collective Encyclopedia. Since the beginning, Wikipedia aims at developing, maintaining and protecting open content to the public free of charge. As a project to allow contribution on online articles, when a user logs in, he or she will have a user-page to introduce themselves; when they make a revision on articles, their edit would be recorded and listed in the article’s version history where one can highlight differences between versions.

However, opinions are highly mixed regarding to what extent has Wikipedia reached this goal as well as whether the content is of good quality. It is worth noting that Wikipedia presents a model of "democratized information," including collective wisdom but not excluding inaccuracies, bias and even vandalisms, purposefully or not. In a way, studying the Wikipedia revision history can be more than studying the accumulation and transformation of knowledge, but rather a documentation of human behaviors when each user has the power of controlling a page accessible to almost the entire world.



I have four datasets which are collected, analyzed and published openly by Wikimedia Foundation, they are respectively:

  • The number of daily total edits from April 2011 to March 2021
    (e.g. 2011-04-28 | 163106 edits)

  • Active editors by country from 2018 to 2021
    (e.g. US | 26010 editors | 2018)

  • Most edited 100 pages every month from 2011 to 2021
    (e.g. page “Osama bin Laden” | 1542 edits | rank 23 | 2011-5 )

  • Detailed revision history of article “World War II” from Dec 2018 to April 2021
    (e.g. user name, timestamp, a short comment why he/she made the change)

    Outcome

    Together, this project takes its users on an adventure to explore Wikipedia in a macro-to-micro perspective, providing a fresh point of view to understand this digital tool existing everywhere in our life.
